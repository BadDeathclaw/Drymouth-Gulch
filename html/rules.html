Ignorance is not a excuse, read the rules.

0. The rules are the rules. Follow the spirit of the rules and use common sense, and you should have no problems.

0.1 Know your faction lore and IC specific rules. If you play as any aligned faction, consider the server rules, superior to Lore.

1. Don't be a dick. Play for fun, not to win.

2. Do not use information gained outside of in character means. No Meta.

3. Do not say in character (IC) things in the out of character (OOC) chat channel. Keep it IC.

4. The role you spawn in as is the role you should play, joining as a role to desert or otherwise not play it, gets you a jobban.

5. In-game administration rulings are final. If you have a problem with the ruling, take it to another member of staff. Fishing for admins is not recommended.

6. Faction Metagaming. Do not move to the enemy factions base until at least 40 minutes into the round. Claiming to have ‘found it’ is a bad excuse, and isn’t going to work.

7. Bad Deathclaw has a focus towards roleplay. Roleplay a believable character that could exist in the Fallout universe. Shitty memes and low roleplay gimmicks will not end well.

8. Do not be unnecessarily hostile. Arguments are fine, being an asshole for no reason isn't. This includes taking it to OOC chat and making trouble.

9. Do not attack or harm AFK/SSD players. You are free to take their items, but not attack them.

10. No rule lawyering.


B. Approach, Motivation and Escalation

1. Do not attack someone wordlessly.

2. Establish hostility before attacking. This means using your words. Wait for them to react, whether words, brandishing a weapon, violence, or compliance.

3. Escalation comes with reasoning and is mostly limited by role background. You shouldn’t see Wastelanders and Settlers being mass-murderers, but Raiders are more likely.

4. Fleeing confrontation constitutes escalation.

5. If you suspect the other player is going to fail to escalate properly, it’s better to let it pan out, then shoot first and have -you- look like the villain.


C. General Conflict

If a player wrongs you (theft, breaks into your shop, etc, you may retaliate in any way you see fit as long as it’s appropriate according to your role.

You can rob, be robbed, defend yourself, murder or be murdered, as long as escalation is adhered to, and you have an IC Reason.

If a player tries to do this wordlessly and you’re not able to catch them in the act or say something before they’ve done it, try and get a screenshot or the like, otherwise it’s a very hard situation to judge from an admin standpoint.

You may instigate conflict with another player within reasoning and motivation. You can't rob and rape a random victim while unprovoked as a Wastelander or Settler, but you could as a Raider, if you follow proper escalation.
Stay true to your chosen position and mindset, and always remember to escalate appropriately.


C.1. NCR and Legion Conflict

Given that approach rules are followed combat is allowed at any point;
And is expected to be initiated, the NCR and Legion are at war.

To engage with your enemy at every point isn't required but it is entirely within reason.

Retreating is fine if you can’t win, but remember, actively avoiding escalation looks bad from the admins perspective.

Escalation rules apply.


D. Alignment and Siding

1.Wastelanders and raiders are allowed to join and side with any of the factions.

2. No hostile action is allowed to be taken while siding with a faction unless you are in their full uniform and attire. Unless you express your verbal allegiance during escalation, you will not be considered valid.

3. When you have joined a faction, you should wear as much of their uniform as is available, or verbally side with them, when situations are escalating.

4. All NCR/BOS/Legion Roles, are beholden to IC Rules, and in the case of their Leaders/Higher Ranks, OOC Rules.

5. Repeatedly breaking IC rules can result in admin intervention.


E. Uniforms and Armor

1. Faction uniforms are to be kept at all times unless deserting or infiltrating non-lethally.
(Expect consequences if you desert your respective faction!)

2. Faction armor should only be changed out of for the purposes of infiltration.

3. Faction uniforms can be used by anyone, but fall under these rules, and normal escalation rules. No surprise murders, no matter how much you like.

4. If dressed as a member of a faction, you are treated as one for matters of escalation and conflict.

5. When infiltrating, it is important to let staff know when you do so. It allows them to be aware of it before issues occur.

H. The Brotherhood of Steel Rules
Brotherhood Chain of Command varies, based on the personnel Present.
Paladin/Head Scribe.
Knight/Scribe.
Initiate Knight/Initiate Scribe.

0. This is the West Coast Chapter/Brotherhood, the East Chapter should only make an appearance through events.

1. The Brotherhood of Steel is held to a much higher standard than anything else on the server. As such as the rules are harder to follow, and the repercussions are more severe.

1.1 The Brotherhood have an established Chain of Command, known as the Chain That Binds. This system prevents Paladins from ordering around initiates assigned to a Scribe or Knight, unless no Scribes or Knights are responsive.

1.2 The Paladin is responsible for all operations relating to the Security of the Bunker, as well as any personnel entering or leaving.

1.3 The Head Scribe is responsible for the internal operations of the Bunker, relating to research, personnel management, and interior design, where security is not a concern.

1.4. When the chain that binds cannot be followed due to a lack of personnel, the Paladin retains general command.

2. The Brotherhood of Steel values advanced technology, and advanced technology only. They are not interested in toasters. They are interested in power armor and stockpiles of energy weapons.

3. The Brotherhood of Steel is an isolationist faction, and as such only trades for technology they cannot otherwise obtain, as well as vital resources to their survival.

5. The Brotherhood of Steel very rarely takes on new recruits, typically those who have proven themselves to the Brotherhood through some extraordinary manner.

6. They do not fiddle around with genetic tampering, diseases, and viruses. They may research it (i.e monkey), but not expose players to it or ever release it to the public.

7. No Ghouls are permitted within the ranks of the BoS.

8. The BoS does not engage in violence that is beyond what is necessary to achieve their goals. They are not violent conquerors, nor are they wasteland saviors. Direct aggressive behavior should be limited to acquiring dangerous/important technology, combating sworn enemies of the BoS (Particularly mutants, Enclave remnants), protecting themselves or their tech, or reacting to violent instigation from other factions/wastelanders.


I. The Vault Rules

0. The vault has no knowledge of the outside world, including if it is safe or not to open their doors.

1. The way they act depends on the Overseer and his directives. The Overseer is not comparable to the Captain in other servers; the Overseer wields much greater power and influence over his subjects, and his word is law. Overseer players must use their power wisely and not forget rule 1: Don't be a dick.

2. The Wasteland is a big scary place for the Vault Dwellers, so expect to roleplay as a character completely scared of the Wasteland and biased to the Vault as years of /Vault propaganda/ and listen to the Overseer. Dwellers shouldn’t rebel, unless if something/certain someone (i.e Overseer and his decisions) threatens their precious lives.

3. Officers are supposed to keep the Dwellers safe, and only neutralize those that pose an actual threat. Those who don’t and are convicted of minor crimes, are to head to the Brig to serve time.

4. Leaving the Vault is not allowed unless you have absolutely no other option.

5. No ghouls are allowed to spawn as a vault dweller.

Notes:
The vault may have an experiment chosen for it by the overseer, anything that is potentially rulebreaking should be brought to active staff to prevent needless repercussions.
Vault experiments may also be provided by Staff, at their leisure.

Wanting to leave the Vault for any other reason should be taken to admins for case by case judgement.
